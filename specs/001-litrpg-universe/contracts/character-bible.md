# Contract: Character Bible

**Deliverable**: `universe/characters/`
**Purpose**: Comprehensive reference for all major characters

---

## Required Documents

### 1. protagonist.md

**Content Requirements**:

#### Identity
- [ ] Full name
- [ ] Age at series start
- [ ] Physical description
- [ ] Distinguishing features

#### Background
- [ ] Pre-apocalypse career details (SWE/Robotics specifics)
- [ ] Family situation
- [ ] Relationship history (recent divorce/breakup)
- [ ] Where they were when games began
- [ ] What they lost

#### Personality
- [ ] Core traits (5-7 defining characteristics)
- [ ] Strengths (competencies)
- [ ] Flaws (must be genuine, not "too smart")
- [ ] Moral line (what they won't do)
- [ ] Coping mechanisms (dark humor specifically)

#### Voice
- [ ] 10+ sample internal monologue passages
- [ ] Dialogue patterns and verbal tics
- [ ] How voice changes under stress
- [ ] How voice evolves across series

#### Arc
- [ ] Book 1 state and transformation
- [ ] Book 2 state and transformation
- [ ] Book 3 state and transformation
- [ ] Book 4 state and transformation
- [ ] Book 5 state and resolution

**Word Count**: 3,000-5,000 words

---

### 2. companions/ (directory)

**Required Files** (minimum 3):
- [ ] `companion-1.md` - Primary non-human companion (emotional anchor)
- [ ] `companion-2.md` - Human ally (potential betrayer or departure)
- [ ] `companion-3.md` - Complex ally (enemy-turned-friend or vice versa)

**Per-Companion Content**:
- Name and designation
- Species and physical description
- How they meet protagonist
- Relationship dynamic
- Independent goals
- Personal arc
- Fate (survives/dies/departs)
- Key scenes together

**Word Count**: 1,000-2,000 words per companion

---

### 3. antagonists/ (directory)

**Required Files** (minimum 3):
- [ ] `antagonist-1.md` - Personal threat (early books)
- [ ] `antagonist-2.md` - Faction threat (mid books)
- [ ] `antagonist-3.md` - System-level threat (late books)

**Per-Antagonist Content**:
- Name and designation
- Species and affiliation
- Motivation (must be comprehensible)
- Methods and resources
- Relationship to protagonist
- Threat escalation across series
- Redeemability potential
- Ultimate fate

**Word Count**: 800-1,500 words per antagonist

---

### 4. supporting/ (directory)

**Required Files** (minimum 5):
- [ ] Recurring allies (non-companion level)
- [ ] Faction representatives
- [ ] Mentors/information sources
- [ ] Comic relief characters
- [ ] Doomed characters (set up for impactful loss)

**Per-Character Content**:
- Name and role
- Brief background
- Relationship to protagonist
- Purpose in narrative
- Appearance schedule (which books)

**Word Count**: 300-600 words per supporting character

---

## Voice Consistency Validation

### Protagonist Voice Checklist

- [ ] Profane but not gratuitous
- [ ] Sarcastic without being mean-spirited
- [ ] Self-deprecating without being pathetic
- [ ] Technical thinking visible in observations
- [ ] Humor used as coping mechanism
- [ ] Vulnerability beneath the jokes

### Differentiation Test

For each major character, answer:
- What verbal tic or pattern distinguishes them?
- How would you identify them from dialogue alone?
- How do they react differently to the same situation?

---

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Protagonist fully defined with voice samples
- [ ] All companion types present
- [ ] Antagonist escalation clear
- [ ] Voice differentiation verified
- [ ] Constitution Principles II & III validated
- [ ] All characters have independent motivations
