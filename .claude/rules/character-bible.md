# Character Bible Rules

**Applies to**: `universe/characters/**/*.md`

## Protagonist File (`protagonist.md`)

### Required Sections

**Identity**
- Full name, age, physical description, distinguishing features

**Background**
- Pre-apocalypse career (SWE/Robotics specifics)
- Family situation
- Relationship history
- Where they were when games began
- What they lost

**Personality**
- Core traits (5-7 defining characteristics)
- Strengths (competencies)
- Flaws (must be genuine, not "too smart")
- Moral line (what they won't do)
- Coping mechanisms (dark humor primary)

**Voice**
- 10+ sample internal monologue passages
- Dialogue patterns and verbal tics
- How voice changes under stress
- How voice evolves across series

**Arc**
- Per-book state and transformation (Books 1-5)

Word count: 3,000-5,000 words

## Companion Files (`companions/*.md`)

Required sections:
- Name and designation
- Species and physical description
- How they meet protagonist
- Relationship dynamic
- Independent goals (not dependent on protagonist)
- Personal arc
- Fate (survives/dies/departs)
- Key scenes together

Word count: 1,000-2,000 words per companion

## Antagonist Files (`antagonists/*.md`)

Required sections:
- Name and designation
- Species and affiliation
- Motivation (must be comprehensible)
- Methods and resources
- Relationship to protagonist
- Threat escalation across series
- Redeemability potential
- Ultimate fate

Word count: 800-1,500 words per antagonist

## Supporting Cast Files (`supporting/*.md`)

Required sections:
- Name and role
- Brief background
- Relationship to protagonist
- Purpose in narrative
- Appearance schedule (which books)

Word count: 300-600 words per supporting character

## Voice Consistency Validation

### Marcus Chen Voice Checklist
- [ ] Profane but not gratuitous
- [ ] Sarcastic without being mean-spirited
- [ ] Self-deprecating without being pathetic
- [ ] Technical thinking visible in observations
- [ ] Humor used as coping mechanism
- [ ] Vulnerability beneath the jokes

### Differentiation Test

For each major character, verify:
- What verbal tic or pattern distinguishes them?
- Could you identify them from dialogue alone?
- How do they react differently to the same situation?

## Constitution Alignment

- Principle II (Voice Consistency) - verified
- Principle III (Protagonist Identity) - verified
- All characters have independent motivations
