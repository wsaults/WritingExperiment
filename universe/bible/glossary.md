# Universe Glossary

## Canonical Terminology

All terms listed are approved for use in the series. Terms marked with **[CORE]** appear frequently and should be used consistently. Terms marked with **[TECHNICAL]** appear in specific contexts. Terms marked with **[COLLOQUIAL]** are informal usage.

---

### A

**Arbiter, The** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: AR-bih-ter*
The decision-making intelligence within the System that manages game rules, progression, and rewards. Communicates through sardonic text notifications. Whether the Arbiter has genuine personality or simulates it is unknown.
*Cross-reference: System, Lattice, Archive*

**Archive, The** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: AR-kive*
The System component that records every contestant's consciousness. Official purpose is "entertainment replay"; actual purpose is speculated. Every contestant who enters a dungeon is archived.
*Cross-reference: System, Arbiter*

**Ascension** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: ah-SEN-shun*
The theoretical endpoint of game progression—reaching a level of System authority that removes contestants from normal participation. What happens after ascension is unknown; no one has returned to describe it.
*Cross-reference: Progression, Levels*

**Attention Units (AU)** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: A-U (spell out)*
Base currency of the entertainment economy, generated when sapient beings watch entertainment. More engaged viewing generates premium AU.
*Cross-reference: Stake Tokens, Resonance Credits*

---

### B

**Build** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
A contestant's combination of stats, class, skills, and equipment that defines their capabilities. Theorycrafting optimal builds is a popular viewer activity.
*Cross-reference: Class, Stats, Skills*

---

### C

**Core Worlds** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
The densest, oldest, and most developed region of galactic civilization. Contains the original territories of the twelve founder species, the Consortium's central infrastructure, and the highest concentration of entertainment production facilities. Dominated by the Old Guard faction.
*Cross-reference: Old Guard, Consortium, Founders, Galactic Regions*

**Class** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
A categorical designation that defines a contestant's primary capability domain. Assigned by the System based on initial performance and choices. May evolve through progression.
*Cross-reference: Build, Progression*

**Compact, The Founding** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: COM-pact*
The agreement between the twelve founder species that established the games as an alternative to direct warfare. Still technically in force; still technically violated regularly.
*Cross-reference: Founders, Old Guard*

**Consortium of Emergent Entertainment** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: con-SOR-tee-um*
The governing body for all sanctioned games. Dominated by founder species but includes observer representatives from non-founders. Abbreviated as "the Consortium."
*Cross-reference: Old Guard, Founders*

**Contestant** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: con-TEST-ant*
Any sapient being participating in the games, whether voluntarily or through harvesting. The games' primary content producers and victims.
*Cross-reference: Games, Dungeon*

**Cycle** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: SY-kul*
Standard galactic time unit, approximately equivalent to 31.4 Earth years. Used for historical dating (e.g., "Cycle 2,300,000").
*Cross-reference: (none)*

---

### D

**Dungeon** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: DUN-jun*
A pocket dimension designed as a game environment. Contains challenges, monsters, rewards, and operates under negotiated physics. Each dungeon has unique themes and rules.
*Cross-reference: Pocket Dimension, Negotiated Physics, Dungeon Space*

**Dungeon Space** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: DUN-jun space*
The dimensional territory occupied by a dungeon, as distinct from baseline reality. Physics within dungeon space are System-controlled.
*Cross-reference: Baseline Reality, Substrate*

---

### E

**Entertainment Viability** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Assessment of a species' potential to generate entertainment value as contestants. Based on factors including cognitive creativity, emotional transparency, and survival instinct.
*Cross-reference: Harvesting, Contestant*

**Extraction** [COLLOQUIAL]
*Pronunciation: ex-TRAK-shun*
Informal term for removing contestants from dungeons before natural completion. May be negotiated by sponsors or facilitated by outside parties.
*Cross-reference: Sponsor, Liberation Collective*

---

### F

**Faction** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: FAK-shun*
An organized group pursuing shared interests within game politics. Major factions include the Old Guard, Liberation Collective, Horizon Syndicate, and others.
*Cross-reference: Old Guard, Liberation Collective, Horizon Syndicate*

**Founders / Founder Species** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: FOUN-ders*
The twelve species that created (or claim to have created) the System and established the games. Enjoy preferential treatment within Consortium structures.
*Cross-reference: Compact, Old Guard, Consortium*

---

### G

**Games, The** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
The collective entertainment system that harvests sapient beings from primitive species and places them in dungeons for galactic audience entertainment. The central institution of galactic civilization.
*Cross-reference: Consortium, Dungeon, Contestant*

---

### H

**Harvesting** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: HAR-vest-ing*
The process of collecting sapient beings from their home planets for game participation. Methods vary; consent is not obtained.
*Cross-reference: Contestant, Entertainment Viability*

**Horizon Syndicate** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: hoh-RY-zon SIN-dih-kit*
Profit-focused entertainment conglomerate that operates as alternative to Old Guard monopolies. Faction-neutral, purely transactional.
*Cross-reference: Faction, Old Guard*

---

### I

**Interface** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: IN-ter-face*
The System's communication method with contestants—text boxes and notifications projected into visual perception. Also used to mean the Audience Interface component.
*Cross-reference: System, Arbiter*

**Inventory** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: IN-ven-tor-ee*
System-managed storage space for contestant items. Physical items converted to data storage and retrieved on demand. Capacity limited by System allocation.
*Cross-reference: System, Build*

---

### K

**Kindari** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: kin-DAR-ee*
Memory-sharing moth-like species. Former contestants, now observers. Core of Liberation Collective. Known for emotional advocacy and witness-bearing.
*Cross-reference: Liberation Collective, Species Profiles*

---

### L

**Lattice, The** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: LAT-iss*
The dimensional substrate underlying physical reality, enabling System manipulation of local physics. The mechanism by which "magic" operates.
*Cross-reference: Substrate, System, Negotiated Physics*

**Level** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Numerical representation of a contestant's progression and capability within the System. Higher levels grant access to more powerful skills and greater physics-manipulation authority.
*Cross-reference: Progression, Stats, Skills*

**Liberation Collective** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: lib-er-AY-shun col-LEK-tiv*
Contestant-rights faction advocating for game abolition. Morally uncompromising but politically weak. Primarily Kindari-supported.
*Cross-reference: Kindari, Faction*

---

### M

**Monster** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Substrate constructs created by the System to populate dungeons. Technically "real" within dungeon space but exist only as game content. Their suffering's moral status is debated.
*Cross-reference: Dungeon, Substrate*

---

### N

**Negotiated Physics** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: neh-GO-shee-ay-ted*
Local reality rules established by the System within dungeon spaces. Dungeons can have physics that differ from baseline reality, enabling "magic" effects.
*Cross-reference: Dungeon Space, Lattice, Baseline Reality*

---

### O

**Observers, The** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: ob-ZER-vers*
Shadowy faction (or conspiracy, or pattern-matching error) that watches the games for unknown purposes. Identity, composition, and goals are all unknown.
*Cross-reference: Faction*

**Old Guard** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Consortium faction representing founder species interests. Dominant political force. Opposes changes that threaten founder privilege.
*Cross-reference: Founders, Consortium, Faction*

---

### P

**Pocket Dimension** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: as English*
A contained space carved from the substrate for specific purposes. Dungeons exist within pocket dimensions.
*Cross-reference: Dungeon, Substrate, Dungeon Space*

**Progression** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: pro-GRESH-un*
A contestant's advancement through the game system—accumulating levels, stats, skills, and capabilities. Central to LitRPG mechanics.
*Cross-reference: Level, Build, Stats*

---

### Q

**Quarantine Zones** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Regions where something went catastrophically wrong—the Substrate Rebellion's aftermath, failed experiments, spaces where reality itself is unstable. Entry is prohibited; enforcement is lethal. No species claims them; the Consortium maintains only warning beacons and patrol vessels.
*Cross-reference: Substrate Rebellion, Galactic Regions*

**Quorathi** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: kwor-ATH-ee*
Jellyfish-like observer species obsessed with documentation. Study games obsessively. Fascinated by humans.
*Cross-reference: Species Profiles*

---

### R

**Refuge Worlds** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Territories occupied by species who were once contestants themselves—harvested, used, and eventually released as "non-viable entertainment." The Kindari are the most prominent residents. Coalition governance based on shared trauma; the Liberation Collective originated here. Hosts almost no dungeon content by deliberate choice.
*Cross-reference: Kindari, Liberation Collective, Galactic Regions*

**Resonance Credits (RC)** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: R-C (spell out)*
Rare currency generated when entertainment creates genuine emotional impact across species boundaries. The most valuable entertainment currency.
*Cross-reference: Attention Units, Stake Tokens*

---

### S

**Syndicate Reaches** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: SIN-dih-kit REECH-ez*
A loose collection of systems where Consortium authority is negotiable and the Horizon Syndicate holds practical power. Known for decentralized governance, dynamic markets, and experimental entertainment content. Earth exists in a transitional zone between Syndicate Reaches and uncharted space.
*Cross-reference: Horizon Syndicate, Galactic Regions*

**Skills** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Specific capabilities granted by the System that enable contestants to perform actions impossible under baseline physics. Interface between contestant intent and System physics-manipulation.
*Cross-reference: Build, System, Level*

**Soul Anchor** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: as English*
The persistent identity pattern the System grants contestants upon dungeon entry. Enables all game mechanics and permits resurrection under specific circumstances.
*Cross-reference: System, Archive*

**Sponsor** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Entities that invest resources in contestants in exchange for various returns—entertainment value, political influence, or economic gain. Relationship can be exploitative, protective, or transactional.
*Cross-reference: Faction, Contestant*

**Stake Tokens (ST)** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: S-T (spell out)*
Currency created through betting and investment on game outcomes. High-risk bets generate premium tokens.
*Cross-reference: Attention Units, Resonance Credits*

**Stats** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
Numerical representations of contestant capabilities—strength, intelligence, agility, etc. System-assigned and System-tracked. Foundation of build theory.
*Cross-reference: Build, Progression, Level*

**Substrate** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: SUB-strayt*
The dimensional fabric underlying physical reality. What humans perceive as "dark matter" and "dark energy" are crude approximations. Advanced civilizations operate within the substrate.
*Cross-reference: Lattice, Pocket Dimension*

**Substrate Rebellion** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: SUB-strayt reh-BELL-yun*
A catastrophic event approximately 500,000 cycles ago when unknown parties attempted to hack the System itself. The failure resulted in the quarantine of an entire stellar cluster—the Rebellion Scar. No survivors. The System's response suggests it doesn't appreciate modification attempts.
*Cross-reference: Quarantine Zones, System*

**System, The** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: as English*
The omnipresent game-management infrastructure. Includes the Lattice, Arbiter, Archive, and Audience Interface. Origin unclear. Possibly more than it appears.
*Cross-reference: Arbiter, Archive, Lattice*

---

### T

**Thirteenth Founder** [TECHNICAL]
*Pronunciation: as English*
A forbidden topic in galactic politics. Official history states twelve species founded the Consortium, but architectural anomalies, linguistic traces, and Velthrani genealogical gaps suggest a thirteenth founding species existed. The Velthrani are uniquely hostile to any inquiry on this subject. What happened to the Thirteenth Founder—betrayal, imprisonment, transformation—remains one of the galaxy's most dangerous mysteries.
*Cross-reference: Founders, Velthrani, Archive*

**Thex Collective** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: THEKS*
Silicon-based beetle species that processes most game-related transactions. Purely economic motivations. Faction-neutral.
*Cross-reference: Species Profiles*

---

### V

**Velthrani** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: vel-THRAN-ee*
Serpentine founding species. Dominant Old Guard voice. Patient, strategic, hostile to human success.
*Cross-reference: Old Guard, Founders, Species Profiles*

**Vorrax** [CORE]
*Pronunciation: VOR-racks*
Species absorbed by the System during a catastrophic event. Can shift between physical and semi-digital states. Unpredictable relationships with everything.
*Cross-reference: Species Profiles, Substrate*

## Deprecated/Rejected Terminology

The following terms have been considered and rejected. Do NOT use these in the series:

| Rejected Term | Reason | Use Instead |
|---------------|--------|-------------|
| "Players" | Too video-gamey, reduces stakes | "Contestants" |
| "Game Masters" | Implies benevolent oversight | "The System" or "The Arbiter" |
| "XP/Experience Points" | Too D&D-specific | "Progression" or describe advancement |
| "NPCs" | Implies monsters aren't real | "Monsters" or "Dungeon Creatures" |
| "Mana" | Too generic fantasy | "Energy," "Power," or specific skill costs |
| "Aliens" | From human POV; humans are aliens to them | Species names or "species" |
| "Powers" | Too superhero-adjacent | "Skills" or "Abilities" |
| "Respawn" | Too video-gamey | "Resurrection" or "Revival" |
| "Boss" | Too video-gamey | "Guardian," "Final Challenge," or describe |
| "Loot" | Too video-gamey | "Rewards," "Drops," "Spoils" |
