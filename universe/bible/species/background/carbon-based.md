# Background Species: Carbon-Based

Species in this file are Tier 3 (brief reference) entries for scene population and variety.

Includes: Standard organic, mammalian, reptilian, avian, aquatic, insectoid biology types.

---

## 1. Corvex

**Biology**: Avian (corvid-like)
**Classification**: Neutral
**Appearance**: Sleek black-feathered humanoids with sharp beaks and wing-arms. Average 1.6m tall. Iridescent plumage shifts purple-green in light.
**Culture**: Collectors and archivists. Obsessed with shiny objects, which they've elevated to a complex trade economy.
**Narrative Use**: Merchants specializing in rare artifacts; information brokers; scene dressing for markets.
**Quick Hook**: "The Corvex trader's head tilted, one eye fixed on the trinket in my hand. 'Interesting,' they croaked. 'Very interesting.'"

---

## 2. Ursathi

**Biology**: Mammalian (ursine)
**Classification**: Neutral
**Appearance**: Massive bear-like beings, 2.5-3m tall when standing. Dense fur ranging from brown to white. Small ears, powerful limbs.
**Culture**: Hibernation-based society. Work intensely for half the cycle, sleep the other half. Patient traders who never rush deals.
**Narrative Use**: Bodyguards; slow but inevitable pursuit; comic relief (sleepy giants); intimidating presence.
**Quick Hook**: "The Ursathi yawned, revealing teeth that could crush my skull. 'We discuss terms... after my nap.'"

---

## 3. Felixar

**Biology**: Mammalian (feline)
**Classification**: Wildcard
**Appearance**: Lithe cat-like humanoids, 1.7m average. Large eyes with vertical pupils, retractable claws, patterned fur (spotted, striped, or solid).
**Culture**: Fiercely independent. No centralized government—each Felixar is their own nation. Form temporary alliances only.
**Narrative Use**: Assassins; scouts; unreliable allies who might help or hinder based on whim.
**Quick Hook**: "The Felixar smiled—all teeth, no warmth. 'I'll help you. Today. Tomorrow is a different question.'"

---

## 4. Vixari

**Biology**: Mammalian (rodent-like)
**Classification**: Sympathetic
**Appearance**: Small (0.8-1m), with large ears, long whiskers, and quick movements. Fur in earth tones. Constantly twitching, observing.
**Culture**: Scavengers and recyclers. Find value in what others discard. Vast underground warrens where nothing is wasted.
**Narrative Use**: Information from the margins; salvage specialists; allies in desperate circumstances; underestimated.
**Quick Hook**: "The Vixari's nose twitched. 'Throw-aways know things the kept-ones don't. You want to know what the big ones miss?'"

---

## 5. Amphrex

**Biology**: Amphibian
**Classification**: Neutral
**Appearance**: Smooth-skinned humanoids with mottled green-brown coloring. Large eyes, webbed digits, can breathe in air or water. 1.5m average.
**Culture**: Dual-environment society. Consider adaptation the highest virtue. Excellent mediators between species.
**Narrative Use**: Guides through aquatic environments; neutral meeting grounds; adaptable allies.
**Quick Hook**: "The Amphrex blinked slowly—a gesture that meant patience in their culture. 'We live in two worlds. We understand... complications.'"

---

## 6. Arachni

**Biology**: Arachnid
**Classification**: Hostile
**Appearance**: Eight-limbed beings with chitinous exoskeletons. Multiple eyes (usually six). Range from 1-2m. Web-spinners with natural silk production.
**Culture**: Web-based architecture and communication. Territorial. View most other species as either threats or prey.
**Narrative Use**: Dungeon hazards; trap-makers; genuinely creepy encounters; silk as valuable commodity.
**Quick Hook**: "The Arachni didn't speak—they rarely did. But the way all six eyes tracked my movement said enough."

---

## 7. Delphari

**Biology**: Cetacean (dolphin-like)
**Classification**: Sympathetic
**Appearance**: Aquatic humanoids with smooth gray skin, elongated skulls, and echolocation organs. Graceful in water, awkward on land. 1.8m average.
**Culture**: Pod-based society with complex acoustic language. Playful, curious, sometimes dangerously so.
**Narrative Use**: Ocean exploration; sonar/detection capabilities; comic relief (pranks); unexpected allies.
**Quick Hook**: "The Delphari's clicks resolved into words through my translator: 'You move funny. Like a rock that wants to be a fish. We like you.'"

---

## 8. Torthan

**Biology**: Reptilian (chelonian)
**Classification**: Neutral
**Appearance**: Heavily armored humanoids with shell-like backs. Slow-moving but nearly invulnerable. 1.4m tall but can retract to half that.
**Culture**: Patience as religion. Decisions take generations. Their word, once given, is unbreakable because changing it would take too long.
**Narrative Use**: Unbreakable shields; immovable obstacles; contracts that last forever; comedy of slowness.
**Quick Hook**: "The Torthan considered my proposal. Three hours later, they blinked. 'We... are... considering.'"

---

## 9. Serpenthi

**Biology**: Reptilian (serpentine)
**Classification**: Wildcard
**Appearance**: Snake-like beings, 3-4m long, with humanoid upper bodies. No legs—move via undulation. Scales in jewel-tones.
**Culture**: Heat-seekers who value warmth above all. Contracts sealed with coiled embraces. Truth-tellers (they find lies inefficient).
**Narrative Use**: Uncomfortable negotiations; truth detection; environmental hazards in cold; exotic merchants.
**Quick Hook**: "The Serpenthi coiled closer, seeking body heat. 'Your heart races. You are afraid, or lying, or both. Which?'"

---

## 10. Goreth

**Biology**: Mammalian (primate)
**Classification**: Neutral
**Appearance**: Massive ape-like beings, 2-2.5m tall. Silver-backed leaders, darker subordinates. Powerful builds, surprisingly gentle hands.
**Culture**: Strength-hierarchy with strict honor codes. Respect earned through combat or demonstrated wisdom. Protect their troops absolutely.
**Narrative Use**: Mercenary companies; honorable opponents; surprisingly philosophical warriors.
**Quick Hook**: "The Goreth studied me with ancient eyes. 'Small,' they rumbled. 'But small things have surprised me before. Show me what you are.'"

---

## 11. Crustari

**Biology**: Crustacean
**Classification**: Neutral
**Appearance**: Crab-like beings with hard shells, multiple legs, and powerful pincers. 1-1.5m tall when upright. Shells often decorated or armored.
**Culture**: Molt-based society—status resets with each shell-shedding. Masters of salvage and underwater construction.
**Narrative Use**: Engineers; salvage operations; underwater combat; armor/shell crafting.
**Quick Hook**: "The Crustari clicked their pincers—a gesture of greeting or threat, I couldn't tell. 'New shell, new deal. What was promised before... was another me.'"

---

## 12. Bovari

**Biology**: Mammalian (bovine)
**Classification**: Sympathetic
**Appearance**: Large, stocky humanoids with horns, broad faces, and powerful builds. 2m average. Herbivores with patient demeanors.
**Culture**: Agricultural society. Value growth, patience, and community. Slow to anger but devastating when provoked.
**Narrative Use**: Farmers in peaceful scenes; stampede combat; gentle giants; steadfast allies.
**Quick Hook**: "The Bovari's nostrils flared. 'We do not start fights,' they said quietly. 'We finish them.'"

---

## 13. Pachori

**Biology**: Mammalian (pachyderm)
**Classification**: Sympathetic
**Appearance**: Elephant-like humanoids with trunk-like noses and large ears. 2.5-3m tall. Gray, wrinkled skin. Never forget.
**Culture**: Memory-keepers. Oral tradition spanning millions of years. Grief and joy expressed through elaborate ceremonies.
**Narrative Use**: Historians; witnesses to ancient events; emotional weight; unstoppable when charging.
**Quick Hook**: "The Pachori's trunk curled thoughtfully. 'I remember,' they said. 'I remember everything. Including what your species has already forgotten.'"

---

## 14. Cervathi

**Biology**: Mammalian (cervine)
**Classification**: Neutral
**Appearance**: Deer-like humanoids with branching antlers (shed seasonally). Graceful, quick, alert. 1.8m average. Large, watchful eyes.
**Culture**: Flight-over-fight philosophy. Masters of evasion and territory knowledge. Antler-size indicates age and wisdom.
**Narrative Use**: Guides; scouts; prey in hunting scenarios; graceful allies; seasonal events (antler shedding).
**Quick Hook**: "The Cervathi's ears swiveled independently, tracking sounds I couldn't hear. 'Something comes,' they whispered. 'We should not be here when it arrives.'"

---

## 15. Lupari

**Biology**: Mammalian (canine)
**Classification**: Wildcard
**Appearance**: Wolf-like humanoids with keen senses and pack structure. 1.7-1.9m tall. Fur colors vary by region. Prominent fangs.
**Culture**: Pack-loyalty above all. Will die for pack members without hesitation. Outsiders must prove themselves to earn trust.
**Narrative Use**: Tracking; pack tactics in combat; loyalty tests; allies earned through respect.
**Quick Hook**: "The Lupari alpha's eyes glowed in the darkness. 'You are not pack,' they growled. 'But pack can be... earned.'"
